\newcommand{\schlagworte}{Indoor Localization, iBeacons, Particle Filter, Monte Carlo Localization}
\newcommand{\zusammenfassung}{Location-based applications, have a permanent position in our life, but until now the available indoor localization systems haven't evolved yet. Apple introduced at their \acs{WWDC}~2013 a new technology called iBeacon, which are small and cheap \acl{BLE} chips, to enable new location awareness features.

In this work we propose a new indoor self-localization solution based on \acl{PF}, resp.\ \acl{MCL}, by combining \acs{RSS} based distance estimations to deployed beacons, with the user's motion, determined by the smartphones build-in sensors, and by additionally using map information, such as free and occupied space.

As a result, our approach solves the global localization problem, and is also able, to detect and to recover from failure state. During our experiments, we were able estimate a user’s stationary location, with a mean error of 2.29~m. Furthermore, we were able to track a user’s path in our test environment.}
